STARLOCK - COMPLETE STORY AND GAMEPLAY RUNDOWN

CORE PREMISE

The player awakens from routine cryo sleep aboard a damaged spaceship drifting far from home. The ship is divided into a small number of adjacent rooms separated by locked or sealed doors. The player begins in the central cryo sleep room and must gradually restore access to the rest of the ship.

At first the immediate goal is survival: restore power, open locked doors, avoid being vented into space, and bring basic ship systems back online. As the player explores, the larger mystery unfolds: what happened to the rest of the crew?

The four-person crew encountered an unknown alien organism during their mission and brought a specimen back to the ship for testing. While studying it, the organism infected the science officer. Believing them dead and wanting to preserve the body for analysis back home, the rest of the crew sealed the science officer in their space suit and froze them in their cryo pod. Upon seeing what the organism could do, the security officer recognized its potential as a weapon and resolved to flee with a specimen and sell it to the highest bidder. To prevent pursuit, the security officer sabotaged critical ship systems, then took a shuttle and escaped. The captain caught on too late, chased the security officer into the shuttle bay, and was sucked into space when the bay decompressed during launch.

All of this happened while the player, the engineer, was in routine cryo sleep. They wake with no idea what occurred. The events of the story unfold slowly through terminals, logs, environmental clues, and the consequences of the sabotage they encounter room by room.

Unbeknownst to the player, the science officer is not actually dead. The organism is keeping them alive in a transformed, hostile state. Once the player opens their pod to retrieve a keycard, the infected science officer eventually escapes containment and begins stalking the ship. The player must build a weapon from items found across the vessel to eliminate this threat before they can safely travel home.

THE CREW

THE ENGINEER - PLAYER

The engineer is the only crew member still aboard and the only one capable of restoring the ship's systems. They are not the captain and do not begin with full ship authorization. They understand power routing, mechanical repair, tools, and ship systems, but they need codes, keycards, and evidence from the rest of the crew to regain control of the vessel.

REYES - CAPTAIN (DEAD)

Mission commander. Cryo pod 2. Reyes believed the mission had to be completed: the specimens had to be brought home, documented, and properly handled through official channels. When Tarn moved to take the shuttle and destroy the ship, Reyes tried to stop them. Pursued Tarn into the shuttle bay during the launch sequence and was killed when the bay decompressed as the shuttle launched. Reyes' locker in the cryo room contains a coded note — a deliberately obscured record of the dispute with Tarn, hidden so Tarn could not destroy it before leaving. A corresponding locked log on the science lab terminal can only be accessed by scanning this note. In the cryo room pod data, Reyes' pod was opened in a hurry, with warning errors still in the system log.

TARN - SECURITY OFFICER (ESCAPED)

Cryo pod 3. After watching the glitch infect and inhabit Vance, Tarn concluded that bringing the organism back to civilization would risk a catastrophic outbreak. As security officer, Tarn saw destroying the ship — specimens, evidence, and all — as the only responsible course of action. Tarn did not see this as betrayal; they saw it as the only decision that protected other people. Took the shuttle alone, sabotaged critical ship systems first to prevent the ship from following, and left. Tarn's pod was opened cleanly with proper authorization, indicating the decision was made deliberately and in advance. Tarn is the only crew member still alive and uninfected. Whether they were right is something the player must decide for themselves.

VANCE - SCIENCE OFFICER (INHABITED)

Cryo pod 4. Lead researcher on the mission. Vance was the first to attempt direct study of a live glitch specimen in the science lab, experimenting with light and audio stimuli. During one of these sessions the organism responded by entering and inhabiting Vance's body. Vance appeared to die. The remaining crew sealed Vance in their EVA suit and froze them in cryo pod 4. In reality, the glitch is sustaining and animating Vance's body in a transformed, predatory state. Once the player opens the pod and retrieves the keycard, Vance eventually breaks free and becomes a roaming threat aboard the ship. Vance's incomplete specimen scanner remains on the workbench in the science lab.

SHIP LAYOUT

From rear to front:

ENGINEERING -> SHUTTLE BAY -> CRYO SLEEP -> SCIENCE LAB -> BRIDGE

The cryo sleep room is the middle room of the ship. The player initially has possible paths in both directions, but both are blocked:

- Rearward, the shuttle bay is exposed to open space because the security officer launched the shuttle without recycling the doors. The shuttle bay door cannot be opened safely.
- Forward, the science lab door is locked and the ship has limited power.

The player must first solve problems in the cryo sleep room, push forward into the science lab, then continue to the bridge. From the bridge they contact command center, who guides them through the next phase: closing and repressurizing the shuttle bay, and then making their way back through the ship to engineering to repair the engines. Along the way they must contend with the infected science officer and assemble a weapon to deal with that threat.

MAIN GAME FLOW

1. Wake in Cryo Sleep
2. Use the cryo room terminal to identify the crew, pod statuses, and the engineer's situation
3. Repair the sabotaged wiring next to the science officer's pod
4. Use the terminal to open the science officer's pod
5. Recover the science officer's keycard from the suited body
6. Unlock the Science Lab
7. Read Reyes' expedition and experiment logs — learn about the glitch and Vance's infection
8. Scan Reyes' coded note at the log terminal to unlock the dispute log — learn about Tarn's decision and Reyes' death
9. Read Vance's workbench notes — find the specimen storage code
10. Unlock specimen storage — examine all three containers, retrieve the frequency emitter
11. Enter the authorization code at the upgrade terminal — insert keycard and receive upgraded keycard
12. Unlock the Bridge with the upgraded card — this is the trigger point
13. Restore communications and contact command center
14. Receive command center's directive: repair the engines and return home
15. Use bridge controls to close and repressurize the shuttle bay
16. On the way back through the cryo room, discover that Vance's pod is now empty — the body is gone
17. Realize Vance is alive and inhabited, somewhere on the ship
18. Cross the shuttle bay safely
19. Reach Engineering and discover the extent of the sabotage
20. Backtrack through the ship to gather remaining scanner components and engine repair tool components
21. Assemble Vance's scanner — use it to track the infected entity
22. Confront and eliminate the inhabited Vance
23. Restart the engines
24. Return to the Bridge and plot a course home

THE FOUR CRYO PODS

There are four cryo pods in the cryo sleep room. The player begins facing the room's main terminal directly across from the pods.

POD 1 - ENGINEER (PLAYER)

The player wakes from this pod due to the ship's emergency wake protocol. The ship awakened them because they are the only remaining person aboard with the technical ability to repair the vessel.

POD 2 - REYES / CAPTAIN (EMPTY)

Reyes' pod was opened in a hurry, with warning errors still in the system log. Reyes woke when Tarn moved toward the shuttle bay and ran to stop them. Never returned. Before the incident, Reyes left a coded note in their locker — a hidden record of the dispute that Tarn could not read or destroy.

POD 3 - TARN / SECURITY OFFICER (EMPTY)

Tarn's pod was opened cleanly, with proper authorization. The lack of errors or warnings is the strongest indicator that Tarn's decision to leave was premeditated. Tarn woke up, made a choice, and executed it deliberately.

POD 4 - VANCE / SCIENCE OFFICER (SEALED)

This pod contains Vance, sealed inside their EVA suit and frozen in cryo. Vance was placed here after the glitch appeared to kill them in the science lab. The pod's wiring was deliberately sabotaged by Tarn to prevent anyone who survived the cryo cycle from opening it. The pod's medical and quarantine logs confirm the organism was dangerous and invasive.

This pod is the most important object in the cryo room. Repairing the sabotaged wiring is the first major puzzle of the game; opening the pod and recovering the keycard is the path out of the room. When the pod first opens, it reveals exactly what the player expects: a frozen, suited body. No twist, no movement, no horror beat. The body remains in place during all backtracking through the science lab. Only after the player opens the door to the bridge does the body silently disappear from the pod, setting up the first real horror beat the next time the player passes back through the cryo room.

ROOM-BY-ROOM BREAKDOWN

ROOM 1: CRYO SLEEP

Purpose:
The starting room introduces the mystery, establishes the player's vulnerability, teaches the game's interaction model, and gates progress on a clear, focused puzzle: get the keycard out of pod 4.

Initial State:
- Emergency lighting only
- Frost on glass and panels
- Alarm tones repeating at low volume
- Two cryo pods empty (captain, security officer)
- One cryo pod sealed and occupied (science officer in suit)
- Player's pod recently opened
- Main power is down; emergency power partly online
- Door to shuttle bay is sealed because the bay is exposed to space
- Door to science lab is locked and requires a keycard
- A panel of power and control wiring next to pod 4 has been visibly torn open and rerouted

Primary Goals:
- Use the central terminal to identify the crew and read pod statuses
- Repair the sabotaged wiring next to pod 4
- Use the terminal to release pod 4's seal
- Recover the science officer's keycard from the suited body
- Unlock the science lab door

Puzzle Sequence:

1. Terminal Briefing
The player faces a terminal directly across from the cryo pods. The terminal lists the four crew members by name and role, the last cryo cycle timestamp for each pod, and the current status of each pod. Pod 1 reads "Wake protocol complete." Pod 2 and pod 3 read "Emergency open." Pod 4 reads "Sealed - QUARANTINE - external interlock fault." This briefing is the player's first source of names and the first hint that something went wrong.

2. Cryo Wiring Repair
The wiring panel next to pod 4 has been intentionally damaged. Wires have been pulled, swapped, or cut. The player must reconnect the pod's external interlock circuit by matching color-coded leads to labeled terminals, possibly with a small fuse or relay swap. Until this is fixed, the terminal cannot release pod 4.

3. Pod Release
With wiring restored, the player returns to the central terminal and triggers the unseal sequence on pod 4. The pod hisses, frost vents, and the suited science officer is revealed. They appear motionless and dead. There is no fakeout here - the body is, as far as the player and the game's framing are concerned, a corpse.

4. Keycard Retrieval
The player approaches the open pod and takes the science officer's keycard from a clip on the suit. This action should land with weight - it is the first time the player physically touches a crew member's body - but it should not register as a horror beat. The body stays where it is.

5. Science Lab Door
The keycard unlocks the door at the front of the cryo room. The player advances into the science lab.

Important Clues Available in This Room:
- Two pods are empty.
- Pod 2 was opened in a hurry, pod 3 was opened cleanly.
- The fourth crew member was placed in cryo under quarantine status, not normal sleep.
- The pod next to the only body had its wiring deliberately torn out.
- The shuttle bay is currently open to space.
- Someone disabled local power and tampered with pod 4 before the player woke.

Story Beat:
The player understands that the ship was not damaged by an accident. Someone among the crew was working against the others, and the science officer's pod was deliberately sabotaged to keep them - or their keycard - out of reach.

ROOM 2: SCIENCE LAB

Purpose:
The science lab is the heart of the mystery. It is where Vance studied the glitch, where the infection happened, and where the argument between Reyes and Tarn became irreversible. It is also where the player must solve four wall-scale puzzles before they can reach the bridge: read Reyes' logs, solve the card upgrade terminal, investigate the specimen storage, and begin assembling Vance's unfinished scanner. The room answers most of the game's first-half questions — and raises the second half's central one.

THE ORGANISM: THE GLITCH

The organism encountered by the crew during the mission exists as a cluster of small, translucent squares. Their movement and behavior resembles a visual artifact — a digital glitch made physical. They do not appear biological in any conventional sense. They are semi-transparent, float slightly, and shift arrangement in response to stimuli. Up close, each square appears hollow or crystalline. The crew had no existing framework for classifying them. Vance cataloged them as glitches because that was the closest analogy available.

What the glitch does: it can enter a living host through sustained proximity and sensory stimulus. Once inside, it does not kill the host — it inhabits them. The host's body is restructured to serve the organism. Vance is the only confirmed case. The full mechanism is not understood.

Initial State:
- Lab is locked down; biological hazard warnings are lit
- Evidence of Vance's workbench experiments is visible but frozen mid-process
- Specimen storage unit on the back wall is sealed; one container is visibly cracked
- Vance's unfinished scanner sits on the workbench, incomplete and inert
- The log terminal has power; two logs are accessible and one is locked behind a document scanner slot

WALL LAYOUT

The four walls of the science lab each have a primary function. The player enters facing the bridge door. Turning clockwise: logs, specimens, workbench.

WALL A — BRIDGE DOOR + CARD UPGRADE TERMINAL

The bridge door is on this wall. To the side of the door is the lab's access control terminal, which includes a card read/write machine. This is where the player upgrades their keycard to senior crew clearance in order to open the bridge door.

The terminal requires the player to solve an authorization puzzle before it will accept a card for upgrading. The puzzle is a 4-digit code entry. The code — mission authorization number 0743 — is embedded in Reyes' Log 1 on the adjacent wall. This creates a soft dependency: the player needs to read the logs before they can upgrade the card.

Once the correct code is entered, the terminal prompts the player to insert their crew keycard. The card is ejected a moment later with upgraded clearance: SENIOR CREW. The bridge door reader now accepts it.

WALL B — REYES' LOG TERMINAL (turn right from bridge door)

A research terminal with a built-in document scanner slot. Three log entries are stored here, authored by Reyes in the captain's capacity as mission commander.

Log 1 — Expedition Record (unlocked):
Reyes describes the survey mission to the planet surface. The crew located what appeared to be debris or crystalline mineral deposits in a low-lying cave system. Closer examination revealed the deposits were not inert — they responded to light, shifted position, and appeared to reorganize in response to audio frequency. Vance identified them as a likely novel organism. The crew collected three specimens in sealed containers and returned to the ship. Reyes notes the mission authorization code (0743) as the filing reference for the collection record. Reyes describes the glitch as beautiful and deeply strange — unlike anything in any scientific record.

Log 2 — Experiment Record (unlocked):
Reyes describes Vance's initial experiments on one of the live specimens. Vance used controlled light and audio stimuli to test the organism's responses. The glitch reacted with increased activity and apparent agitation when exposed to certain audio frequencies. During one session, the organism suddenly moved out of containment and toward Vance directly. What followed was rapid and difficult to document: the glitch appeared to enter Vance's body through sustained proximity, possibly through the respiratory system or skin contact. Vance collapsed. Vital signs became erratic then stabilized at unnatural levels. By all indicators Vance died; by all indicators something was still running the body. Reyes and Tarn sealed Vance in the EVA suit and placed them in cryo pod 4 under quarantine. Reyes notes that the wiring on pod 4 was sabotaged shortly after — by whom, the log does not say.

Log 3 — Dispute Record (LOCKED — requires coded note):
This log is protected by a physical key: Reyes encoded a paper copy of the log's decryption key and hid it in their cryo locker as a backup, knowing Tarn might wipe or lock the digital version before leaving. The player must retrieve the coded note from Reyes' locker in the cryo room and feed it into the scanner slot on this terminal to unlock Log 3.

Contents of Log 3: Reyes describes the argument with Tarn after Vance's infection. Tarn argued that the only safe course was to take the shuttle, destroy the main ship and all specimens, and return alone — no evidence, no organism, no risk to civilization. Reyes refused: abandoning the ship meant abandoning the engineer, who was still in cryo sleep, and destroying all specimens would remove any chance of developing a treatment or understanding the organism. Reyes also believed the proper course was to return to base and report through official channels. Neither Reyes nor Tarn persuaded the other. The log ends with Reyes stating that Tarn has moved toward the shuttle bay and that Reyes is going after them. There is no further entry.

WALL C — SPECIMEN STORAGE (back wall, cryo door side)

Three specimen containers are housed in a sealed storage unit on the back wall. The door to the cryo room is on this wall as well. The storage unit has a control panel that requires an access code before any container can be released for examination.

The access code (3-7-1) is written in Vance's workbench notes on the opposite wall. The player must go to the workbench, read the notes, find the code, and return here to unlock the storage.

Container A — Empty:
The container is clean and sealed, but empty. There is no residue or trace of a specimen. Either this container was never filled, or something was removed deliberately. Tarn's badge was last logged in the lab just before the shuttle launched.

Container B — Cracked:
The containment seal on this container is broken from the inside. This is where the glitch escaped during Vance's experiment. The interior has faint residue — traces of the organism's passage. Examining this container with the completed scanner (later in the game) will confirm residue patterns consistent with the infected entity now loose on the ship.

Container C — Live Specimen:
One glitch specimen remains, contained and active. Through the glass the player can observe it: a shifting cluster of translucent squares, reorganizing slowly, apparently aware of being watched. This container is intact and sealed. It is the only surviving physical specimen on the ship. What the player does with it — or is instructed to do with it by command center — becomes relevant at the end of the game.

WALL D — VANCE'S WORKBENCH (left wall)

Vance's workspace. A partially assembled scanner device sits at the center of the bench surrounded by tools, reference notes, and disassembled components. The scanner was Vance's project for analyzing glitch specimens at a distance — using a combination of focused light and frequency detection to map the organism's position and behavior without direct contact. It was never finished.

Vance's notes on the workbench are readable and include several key details: the specimen storage access code (3-7-1), observations about the glitch's behavior during experiments, and a rough schematic for the scanner's assembly. The schematic shows that the scanner requires four components to function:

Scanner Components:
1. Calibration Lens — a precision optical element. One is partially installed on the workbench already (found in place during the lab visit).
2. Frequency Emitter — a small audio transducer tuned to the organism's reactive range. One is in Container A of the specimen storage (the empty container), placed there by Vance for safekeeping before the incident.
3. Power Cell — a high-density cell compatible with the scanner chassis. One is accessible in the cryo room emergency kit after power is partially restored.
4. Signal Amplifier — a signal-boosting board. Found in the bridge command station during the second visit to the bridge.

Once all four components are gathered and installed, the scanner becomes functional. Its initial use is as a detection tool: it can illuminate biological residue left by the infected Vance, acting as a tracker and revealing where the entity has moved through the ship. This is the player's primary tool for anticipating the threat in the second half of the game.

The scanner's secondary potential — as a weapon — is left open. If the glitch responds with agitation to the audio frequencies Vance used in the experiments, a modified version of the scanner broadcasting at full power might be capable of driving it out of a host or disrupting it directly. This is the subject of a later design decision.

Primary Goals (Science Lab):
- Read Reyes' Log 1 (expedition record) and Log 2 (experiment record)
- Note the mission authorization code from Log 1 (0743)
- Scan Reyes' coded note at the terminal slot to unlock Log 3 (dispute record)
- Enter the authorization code at the upgrade terminal on Wall A
- Insert the crew keycard — receive the upgraded keycard
- Go to the workbench and read Vance's notes — find the storage code (3-7-1)
- Enter the code at the specimen storage panel — examine all three containers
- Retrieve the frequency emitter from Container A (scanner component)
- Note the live specimen in Container C
- Begin scanner assembly at the workbench (calibration lens is already in place)

Important Clues:
- The glitch is not a weapon and not a resource — it is a living thing that inhabits other living things.
- Vance's infection happened during routine experimentation, not through carelessness.
- Container A is empty; Tarn's badge was last logged in the lab just before the shuttle launched.
- Tarn's stated reason for leaving was to protect humanity. The player has no way to confirm or refute this yet.
- Reyes tried to stop Tarn and died for it.
- The only remaining glitch specimen is still alive in Container C.

Story Beat:
The player enters the lab knowing Vance is in pod 4 and something went wrong. They leave knowing exactly what went wrong, who chose to run, who tried to stop them, and why. The argument between Reyes and Tarn is not a story about a villain — it is a story about two people who both believed they were making the right call. The player is left with the residue of that argument and no easy answer. The empty specimen container is a quiet reminder that Tarn may have taken something with them.

ROOM 3: BRIDGE

Purpose:
The bridge is the command and information hub. It lets the player affect the ship's layout by closing the shuttle bay doors, and it is where command center first speaks directly to the player and sets the next major objective.

Initial State:
- Bridge door locked until science lab access is restored
- Navigation offline
- Communications damaged or weak
- Engine status: offline (sabotaged)
- Shuttle bay status: open to vacuum
- Some command logs are encrypted

Primary Goals:
- Restore communications
- Contact command center
- Receive command center's directive (return home; engines must be repaired)
- Access shuttle bay controls
- Close and repressurize the shuttle bay
- Learn more about the captain's final actions

Puzzle Ideas:

1. Communications Alignment Puzzle
Align a damaged antenna or frequency system using star chart data, signal waveforms, or a command beacon coordinate. Successfully aligning communications triggers the first proper transmission with command center.

2. Command Authentication Puzzle
Combine crew IDs, lab quarantine data, and cryo pod records to gain limited bridge access. Full bridge access remains gated for a later visit.

3. Shuttle Bay Door Override
The player cannot simply press a button. They must clear a safety lock, confirm no life signs in the bay, restore power to the door motors, and override an emergency decompression state.

4. Captain's Log Decryption
Some logs require multi-crew authorization. The player can read partial entries that reveal the captain woke in alarm, suspected the security officer, and ran toward the shuttle bay.

Important Clues:
- The shuttle bay was opened during a manual launch sequence initiated from the bay itself.
- The main shuttle is gone.
- The security officer's command badge was used at multiple critical sabotage points.
- The captain's last logged activity is in the shuttle bay corridor.
- A distress message was partially transmitted but never completed.

Command Center Contact:
On first contact, command center is staticky and limited. They confirm the ship is off course, instruct the player to repair the engines, and order the player to close the shuttle bay and proceed to engineering. Their tone is professional but oddly focused on logs and cargo recovery rather than on the missing crew.

Story Beat:
Reaching the bridge is the narrative threshold. The moment the player unlocks the bridge door, the science officer's body silently leaves pod 4. The player will not see this happen. They close the shuttle bay doors, turning the ship's rear section from a death trap into an explorable space, and only on the trip back through the cryo room do they realize the corpse is gone. That delayed discovery is the first true horror beat in the game.

ROOM 4: SHUTTLE BAY

Purpose:
The shuttle bay is the scene of the central crime and the route to engineering. It also reinforces the new threat: the science officer is loose somewhere on the ship.

Initial State After Repressurization:
- Bay doors closed but the room is damaged
- Cargo is scattered from decompression
- Tool lockers are broken or locked
- Shuttle cradle is empty
- Scrape marks, impact damage, and emergency warnings are visible
- A torn safety tether, a glove, or fragments of the captain's gear remain near the bay doors
- Faint trail evidence (frost residue, biological smear) indicates something dragged itself across the floor

Primary Goals:
- Investigate the escape
- Confirm that the security officer launched the shuttle
- Confirm that the captain was sucked into space trying to stop the launch
- Recover the magnetic torque head and other engineering tools
- Open the door to engineering
- Recognize signs that the science officer is moving through the ship

Puzzle Ideas:

1. Cargo Loader Puzzle
Cargo shifted during decompression and blocks access to a locker or the engineering door. The player must use a damaged loading arm or magnetic crane to move crates in the correct order.

2. Shuttle Launch Log Puzzle
The shuttle launch system contains a partial record. The player must restore power or replace a fuse to confirm who launched the shuttle and when. The log identifies the security officer.

3. Tether Evidence Puzzle
The captain's torn safety tether is caught in the bay door mechanism. Recovering and analyzing it confirms that a person was pulled out when the doors opened.

4. Tool Locker Puzzle
A maintenance locker contains the magnetic torque head needed for engineering. The code is tied to shuttle preflight checks, cargo manifest numbers, or the security officer's credentials.

5. Trail Investigation
A faint trail of frost or biological residue suggests something thawed inside the ship and moved through this room. The trail leads toward engineering, the ducts, or back into the cryo corridor.

Important Clues:
- Tarn launched the shuttle.
- Container A in specimen storage was empty — Tarn may have taken a glitch specimen with them.
- Reyes tried to stop the launch and was killed when the doors opened.
- Engineering was disabled before the launch to prevent pursuit.
- Something thawed inside pod 4 and is now moving through the ship.

Story Beat:
The central conflict is fully understood — not as betrayal, but as a disagreement that became catastrophic. Reyes died trying to stop what Tarn genuinely believed was the right thing to do. Vance is no longer in their pod.

ROOM 5: ENGINEERING

Purpose:
Engineering is the final major repair challenge and the venue for the climactic confrontation with the infected science officer. It proves the ship was sabotaged and forces the player to backtrack for both the engine repair tool and the weapon.

Initial State:
- Engine systems offline
- Reactor in safe shutdown
- Coolant system unstable
- Control relays damaged
- Several tools missing
- Engine ignition cannot be restored with standard controls
- Clear evidence of sabotage: cut lines, smashed panels, pulled chips
- Signs of intrusion: scuffs, frost, biological residue

Primary Goals:
- Determine why the engines are offline
- Stabilize the reactor or power core
- Build a custom engine repair tool
- Build an improvised weapon
- Defeat the infected science officer
- Restore propulsion
- Return to the bridge

The Engine Repair Tool:
The player must assemble a custom tool to repair or recalibrate the engine ignition system. This creates a reason to revisit earlier rooms.

Possible Tool Names:
- Engine Recalibration Tool
- Plasma Ignition Spanner
- Drive Core Recalibrator

Engine Repair Tool Components:

1. Insulated Grip - cryo room emergency kit, accessible after restoring power and using a lab reagent or shuttle bay tool.
2. Micro-Focusing Lens - removed from the science lab scanner.
3. Command Authorization Chip - unlocked on the bridge once partial command access is restored.
4. Magnetic Torque Head - recovered from the shuttle bay maintenance locker.
5. High-Density Power Cell - recovered from the empty pod 4, the lab containment unit, or an emergency bridge system.

The Improvised Weapon:
The player must also build something capable of harming the infected science officer. This is a parallel item track that crosses many of the same rooms but uses different parts.

Possible Weapon Names:
- Coilgun Prototype
- Plasma Lance
- Cryo-Igniter

Weapon Components (examples - exact set should be tuned for play):

1. A volatile reagent or pressurized canister from the science lab.
2. An ignition coil or capacitor from engineering.
3. A focusing or directional element repurposed from a shuttle bay tool.
4. A stabilizing housing from the cryo room emergency kit.
5. A trigger or interface from the bridge command station.

Engineering Puzzle Ideas:

1. Coolant Pressure Puzzle
Balance coolant across several valves. Too much pressure causes shutdown; too little overheats the engine core.

2. Relay Replacement Puzzle
Damaged power relays must be replaced or rerouted using components found elsewhere.

3. Tool Assembly Puzzle
Combine parts from multiple rooms into the engine repair tool. The assembled tool may require calibration using a science lab terminal.

4. Reactor Restart Sequence
The final engine restart requires a timed or ordered sequence using clues from engineering labels, bridge diagnostics, and the science officer's pre-infection notes.

5. Sabotage Attribution
The player confirms that the engines were intentionally disabled by the security officer to prevent the ship from following the shuttle.

The Confrontation:
At a key point in engineering - ideally between gathering the last weapon component and the engine restart - the infected science officer attacks. The player must use the assembled weapon to eliminate the threat. The fight is not action-game combat; it is positional, environmental, and puzzle-flavored. Possible mechanics include luring the science officer into a coolant vent, sealing them behind a blast door, overloading them with a specific energy type, or using the cryo system to refreeze them permanently.

Story Beat:
The player restores the engines and eliminates the only remaining trace of the organism aboard the ship. The larger threat - the security officer with their stolen specimen - is somewhere out there, beyond the player's reach.

BACKTRACKING STRUCTURE

The game should not feel like a simple sequence of rooms. Earlier rooms gain new importance as the player acquires tools, access, and knowledge.

Cryo Sleep Revisited:
- Between the science lab and the bridge, the room is unchanged - the corpse is still in pod 4 and any backtracking here is uneventful
- After the bridge door is unlocked, the next return trip reveals pod 4 is empty
- Open the frozen emergency kit (insulated grip, weapon housing)
- Access deeper logs from the now-empty pod 4
- Retrieve a high-density power cell
- Confirm the chronological order of pod openings using bridge authorization

Science Lab Revisited:
- Use the assembled scanner to detect glitch residue from the empty pod 4 and the trail through the lab
- Analyze Container B residue with the scanner (confirms the infected entity is Vance)
- Observe the live specimen in Container C with the scanner — this may reveal the glitch's weakness
- Recover any remaining weapon or tool components from the workbench or storage area

Bridge Revisited:
- Receive clearer command center transmissions
- Unlock additional crew logs after engines are restored
- Use shipwide diagnostics to locate missing components
- Recover the command authorization chip and a weapon trigger interface

Shuttle Bay Revisited:
- Use engineering knowledge to access damaged launch logs
- Retrieve the magnetic torque head and a directional housing for the weapon
- Confirm the escaped shuttle's trajectory

Engineering Revisited:
- First visit reveals the engine sabotage and weapon-relevant components
- Later visit allows tool-assisted repair, the confrontation with the science officer, and the final restart

THE INHABITED VANCE

Behavior:
The inhabited Vance should remain mysterious and intermittent. The game does not need to show them in detail right away. Their presence is established first through environmental signs — the empty pod, frost trails, glitch residue, distant sounds — and only later through direct encounters. The player's assembled scanner becomes their primary tool for tracking and anticipating Vance's movements.

Encounter Pacing:
- Pre-bridge: pod 4 still contains the suited body; backtracking to the cryo room is uneventful
- Trigger: opening the door to the bridge silently empties pod 4
- First return through cryo: empty pod, seals broken outward, faint trail
- Mid-game: ambient signs, brief glimpses, distant sounds, scrabbling in vents
- Late mid-game: environmental hazards traced back to Vance (banged-shut doors, vents pried open, glitch residue that the scanner can read)
- Final act: the climactic confrontation in engineering

Resolution:
Vance must be stopped using the assembled scanner or a weapon derived from it. The game treats this as a tragic necessity — Vance was a victim of the glitch, not an antagonist. Whether the player learns anything from this encounter about how to stop the organism depends on what they discovered in the science lab.

COMMAND CENTER ROLE

Command center should be distant, delayed, and not fully helpful at first. Their signal may be broken, distorted, or limited by the ship's damaged communications.

Early Contact:
- Static-heavy response
- Basic confirmation that the ship is off-course
- Instructions to repair engines and return home
- Request for all available logs

Later Contact:
- Command confirms the escaped shuttle's trajectory
- They ask whether the organism is still aboard
- They may instruct the player to bring the ship - and any remaining specimen material - to a quarantine station
- Or they may instruct the player to return home directly

Possible Tension:
Command center may care more about recovering the organism than saving the player. This can make the final decision more morally complicated and seed sequel possibilities.

KEY STORY REVEAL ORDER

Reveal 1 - Cryo Room:
Three crew members are unaccounted for. Vance is sealed in a sabotaged pod. The cryo records show a deliberate attack on pod 4's wiring. Reyes' locker contains a coded note that makes no sense yet.

Reveal 2 - Science Lab (Logs 1 and 2):
The crew found the glitch on the planet surface and brought specimens aboard. Vance experimented on a live specimen; the glitch entered Vance's body during a session using light and audio stimuli. Vance appeared to die. Reyes and Tarn sealed Vance in the EVA suit and placed them in cryo pod 4.

Reveal 3 - Science Lab (Log 3 — requires coded note):
Tarn believed destroying the ship was the only way to protect other people from the glitch. Reyes refused and tried to stop Tarn. Reyes died in the shuttle bay. Tarn left. Container A is empty — Tarn may have taken a specimen.

Reveal 4 - Science Lab (specimens):
The live glitch in Container C is still active. The cracked Container B is where the infection started. Container A is empty — the specimen that was here is gone.

Reveal 5 - Bridge:
Tarn launched the shuttle manually from the bay. The bay was left open to space. Tarn sabotaged the engines to prevent pursuit. (Opening the bridge door is the silent trigger that empties pod 4 offscreen.)

Reveal 6 - Cryo Room (returning from bridge):
Vance's pod is empty. The seal is broken outward. Something is moving on the ship.

Reveal 7 - Shuttle Bay:
Evidence confirms Reyes was pulled into space at the bay doors. A trail of biological residue leads toward engineering. Using the scanner here confirms it matches Vance's biological signature.

Reveal 8 - Engineering:
Tarn's sabotage is fully visible. The inhabited Vance attacks. The scanner-based weapon ends the confrontation.

Reveal 9 - Final Bridge Return:
The glitch is no longer aboard the main ship — except possibly in the player's memory of what the live specimen in Container C represents. Tarn is somewhere out there, possibly with a specimen. The player must decide what to do.

FINAL ACT

After restoring the engines and eliminating the science officer, the player returns to the bridge. The ship can now move again, communications are clearer, and navigation is partially restored.

The player has enough information to understand what happened:

- Vance was inhabited by the glitch during an experiment and presumed dead.
- Reyes and Tarn sealed Vance in their EVA suit and froze them in cryo pod 4.
- Tarn decided the only safe course was to take the shuttle and destroy the ship.
- Tarn sabotaged ship systems to prevent pursuit and left.
- Reyes tried to stop Tarn and was killed when the shuttle bay decompressed.
- Vance reanimated after the player opened pod 4 and has been dealt with.
- The engines are repaired. Tarn is somewhere out there — possibly with a specimen.

Final Goals:
- Restore navigation
- Confirm contact with command center
- Transmit ship logs
- Plot a course
- Decide what to do next

ENDING OPTIONS

Ending 1 - Return Home
The player returns home with the main ship. The most straightforward survival ending, but Tarn is still out there — possibly with a live specimen.

Ending 2 - Pursue the Shuttle
The player uses the restored engines to follow Tarn's trajectory. Whether to stop Tarn, help them, or learn from them is unresolved. Tarn may not be wrong.

Ending 3 - Divert to Quarantine Station
The player follows command protocol and brings the ship to a quarantine station, including the live specimen in Container C. Evidence is preserved.

Ending 4 - Broadcast the Truth
The player transmits all logs — Reyes' logs, the dispute record, the specimen data — widely. No one can bury this.

Ending 5 - Dark Ending
The player discovers command center already knew about the mission's findings. Tarn may have been acting on someone's orders, or may be heading somewhere they were told to go. The glitch was never just a scientific curiosity.

RECOMMENDED CANON ENDING

The strongest ending is not that the player simply goes home. After restoring the ship and ending the science officer, the player discovers the escaped shuttle's trajectory and finds command center oddly more interested in cargo than in their lost crew.

The player chooses:

- Obey command and return home
- Pursue the stolen organism
- Divert to quarantine and transmit the truth

This creates a strong final choice and leaves the story with tension beyond the ship itself.

GAMEPLAY TONE

Recommended tone:
Lonely sci-fi mystery with rising horror. The first half is investigative and quiet; the second half adds an active, unseen threat.

The game should feel tense and forensic rather than action-heavy. The disaster has already happened. The player walks through the aftermath, reconstructs it through puzzles, and only then confronts what survived it.

Inspirations in tone:
- A locked-room mystery
- A drifting spaceship survival story
- A forensic investigation
- A sci-fi quarantine thriller
- A point-and-click adventure with escape room logic
- A slow-burn creature feature where the creature is mostly off-screen

VISUAL AND AUDIO DIRECTION

Cryo Sleep:
Cold blue lighting, frost, condensation, alarms, muffled mechanical hum. After the mid-game beat, pod 4 stands open and dark, the suit gone, the seals broken outward.

Science Lab:
Sterile whites and greens, cracked containment glass, biological hazard lights, microscope glow, sealed cabinets.

Bridge:
Dark control room, starfield views, flickering screens, static-filled communications, navigation maps.

Shuttle Bay:
Large dark chamber, warning lights, empty shuttle cradle, cargo scattered from decompression, heavy silence after repressurization. Faint trails on the deck plates.

Engineering:
Warm orange emergency lights, exposed machinery, coolant vapor, deep engine sounds, sparking panels. In the final act: motion at the edge of view, then the confrontation.

KEY DESIGN PRINCIPLES

1. Every room should answer one question and create another.
2. Every major puzzle should either unlock progress, reveal story, or both.
3. Backtracking should feel purposeful, not like padding.
4. The player should slowly shift from "Where is everyone?" to "Who caused this?" to "What's still aboard with me?" to "How do I stop what already left?"
5. The organism should remain mysterious. Its effects, value, and danger are more frightening than a full reveal.
6. Pod 4 should be emotionally and mechanically important throughout the game - first as a sealed puzzle, then as a tomb, then as an empty horror.
7. The shuttle bay should be the centerpiece reveal location for the captain and the security officer.
8. The bridge should serve as both midpoint control room and final decision room.
9. The player's inventory should connect the ship together physically and narratively, with the engine repair tool and the weapon as parallel chains.
10. The final act should make the player question whether simply going home is the right thing to do.

SAMPLE PUZZLE ITEM CHAIN

Cryo Room (initial):
- Read terminal briefing
- Repair sabotaged pod 4 wiring
- Open pod 4
- Take science officer's keycard

Science Lab (first visit):
- Read Reyes' Log 1 (expedition — includes mission auth code 0743)
- Read Reyes' Log 2 (Vance's infection)
- Scan coded note from Reyes' locker → unlock Log 3 (the Reyes/Tarn dispute)
- Go to Vance's workbench → read notes → find specimen storage code (3-7-1)
- Unlock specimen storage → examine all three containers
- Retrieve frequency emitter from Container A (scanner component 2 of 4)
- Note calibration lens already on workbench (scanner component 1 of 4)
- Enter code 0743 at the upgrade terminal → insert keycard → receive upgraded keycard
- Use upgraded keycard at bridge door → bridge unlocked

Bridge:
- Restore communications
- Contact command center
- Close shuttle bay doors
- Gain partial command authorization
- Recover trigger interface (weapon component)

Cryo Room (revisit, after bridge unlocked):
- Discover pod 4 is empty (the corpse was still there on any earlier backtrack)
- Open emergency kit (insulated grip + weapon housing)
- Recover high-density power cell

Shuttle Bay:
- Move cargo
- Retrieve magnetic torque head
- Recover launch log fragment
- Recover directional housing (weapon component)

Engineering:
- Discover engine sabotage
- Identify final tool and weapon requirements
- Recover ignition coil/capacitor (weapon component)

Backtracking:
- Return to science lab for micro-focusing lens
- Return to bridge for command authorization chip
- Combine remaining components

Engineering Return:
- Assemble engine repair tool
- Assemble weapon
- Confront and eliminate the infected science officer
- Repair engine core
- Restart propulsion

Bridge Finale:
- Plot course
- Contact command
- Decide what to do about the escaped shuttle and the security officer

ONE-SENTENCE SUMMARY

A lone engineer wakes from cryo sleep aboard a sabotaged spaceship, discovers that one crewmate fled with a stolen alien weapon, that the captain died trying to stop them, and that the science officer they thought was dead is now infected and roaming the ship - and must repair the engines and build a weapon to survive long enough to decide what to do with the truth.
